Privacy

Your data isn't the product

Google makes 80% of its revenue from advertising — which means Chrome's job is to learn everything about you. Firefox's job is to protect you.

2,000+
trackers blocked per browsing session on average
Facebook Pixel Google Analytics DoubleClick Twitter Tracker Fingerprint.js Cross-site cookies Cryptominers
  • 🛡️

    Enhanced Tracking Protection — On by default

    Blocks social media trackers, cross-site tracking cookies, fingerprinters, and cryptominers automatically. No settings to hunt for, no opt-in required.

  • 🍪

    Total Cookie Protection

    Every website gets its own isolated cookie jar. Sites cannot use cookies to track you across the web — a feature Chrome promised and then abandoned.

  • 🔒

    Encrypted DNS by default

    DNS-over-HTTPS encrypts your domain lookups so your ISP and network observers can't see which sites you're visiting. Chrome and Edge make you opt in.

  • 👆

    Anti-fingerprinting built in

    In February 2025, both Google and Microsoft were caught allowing advertisers to collect digital fingerprint data — including your IP address. Firefox blocks this by default.

Who's Behind It

A browser built by people,
not by an ad company

Mozilla is a nonprofit. Their mission is internet health — not quarterly earnings. That difference shapes every decision.

🏛️

Nonprofit structure, public interest mission

Mozilla is not beholden to shareholders or ad revenue. Chrome is built by a company that earns 80% of its revenue from advertising — a direct conflict of interest with browser privacy.

🔍

100% open source — anyone can audit it

Firefox's entire codebase is public. Independent security researchers, academics, and journalists can verify every privacy claim. Chrome and Edge are built on closed-source layers.

🌐

Fighting web monopoly — not creating one

Chrome holds 67% of the browser market. When one company controls the browser, it controls the web. Firefox is the only major non-Chromium browser keeping the web ecosystem diverse.

💰

Revenue from search partnerships, not your data

Firefox earns money from search engine deals (Google pays ~$400M/year to be the default). Your browsing history is never sold. Edge and Chrome monetize your behavior directly.
